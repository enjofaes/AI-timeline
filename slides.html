A History of
Artificial Intelligence

From the Turing Machine to AI Agents

Based on a GARP course (Risk and AI)  ·  Open-source timeline

Seven Eras

  • 1936–1998 Foundations & Early Research
  • 2000s Into the Real World
  • 2010s Deep Learning Breakthrough
  • 2015–2017 Ethics Enters the Picture
  • 2018–2020 Governance Takes Shape
  • 2021–2024 Regulation & Generative AI
  • 2025 The Age of AI Agents

1936–1960  Foundations

  • 1936 Alan Turing introduces the Turing Machine — the theoretical foundation of computing. Tech
  • 1943 McCulloch & Pitts publish the first mathematical model of a neuron. Tech
  • 1947–1949 Nuremberg Code, UDHR, and Hebbian learning lay ethical and scientific groundwork. Governance
  • 1950 Turing proposes the Turing Test; Asimov publishes the Three Laws of Robotics. Risk
  • 1955 Newell, Simon & Shaw build the Logic Theorist, the first AI program.
  • 1956 Dartmouth Conference — the term "Artificial Intelligence" is coined by John McCarthy. Tech
  • 1958 Frank Rosenblatt introduces the Perceptron, the first neural network model.
  • 1959 Arthur Samuel builds a self-learning checkers program — early machine learning.

1966–1993  Boom & Bust

  • 1966 ELIZA (Weizenbaum, MIT) — first chatbot; raises early concerns about the illusion of understanding. Risk
  • 1969 Minsky & Papert critique the Perceptron in Perceptrons, freezing neural network research.
1974–1980  ·  First AI Winter Winter
Funding collapses as hype outpaces results. A defining pattern in AI history.
  • 1980 Expert Systems boom — XCON, MYCIN; Japan's Fifth Generation project. Tech
  • 1986 Rumelhart & McClelland popularise backpropagation, reviving neural networks.
1987–1993  ·  Second AI Winter Winter
Expert systems prove brittle and expensive; another funding collapse follows.

1997–2009  Into the World

  • 1997 IBM's Deep Blue defeats Garry Kasparov in chess. Tech
  • 1998 Yann LeCun develops LeNet-5, a CNN for digit recognition.
  • 2004 DARPA Grand Challenge — first autonomous vehicle race; no finisher, but a catalyst.
  • 2005 Stanford's Stanley wins DARPA Grand Challenge (132 miles).
  • 2005 Kurzweil publishes The Singularity Is Near. Risk
  • 2006 Hinton introduces Deep Belief Networks; Fei-Fei Li founds ImageNet. Tech
  • 2009 Google begins self-driving car research; MIRI is founded. Risk

2011–2014  Deep Learning

  • 2011 IBM Watson wins Jeopardy!; Apple launches Siri. Tech
  • 2012 AlexNet (Krizhevsky, Sutskever, Hinton) wins ImageNet — deep learning takes over computer vision. Tech
  • 2013 Google's Word2Vec advances NLP; DeepMind beats Atari games with reinforcement learning.
  • 2014 Google acquires DeepMind.
  • 2014 Ian Goodfellow invents GANs, revolutionising generative AI. Tech
  • 2014 Amazon launches Alexa; Musk & Hawking warn of existential AI risks. Risk
  • 2014 Future of Life Institute founded. Governance

2015–2017  Ethics Arrives

Technology

  • Google open-sources TensorFlow (2015) — democratises deep learning
  • AlphaGo defeats Lee Sedol 4–1 (2016)
  • Google publishes Attention Is All You Need — Transformers (2017)
  • AlphaGo Zero learns entirely without human data (2017)

Governance & Ethics

  • OpenAI founded with AGI safety mission (2015)
  • IEEE Global Initiative on AI Ethics (2016)
  • Microsoft Tay chatbot incident (2016) Risk
  • Tesla Autopilot fatal crash (2016) Risk
  • Asilomar AI Principles — 23 guidelines (2017)
  • China Next-Gen AI Plan; Partnership on AI (2017)

2018–2019  Governance

2018

  • GDPR comes into force — data rights matter Governance
  • EU AI HLEG established
  • Amazon recruiting AI bias scandal Risk
  • Uber self-driving fatality Risk
  • Cambridge Analytica scandal Risk
  • OpenAI releases GPT-1 Tech
  • Google releases BERT; DeepMind: AlphaFold

2019

  • EU AI HLEG: Ethics Guidelines for Trustworthy AI Governance
  • San Francisco bans facial recognition
  • OECD AI Principles — first intergovernmental AI standards
  • OpenAI releases GPT-2, withholds full model citing safety Risk
  • OpenAI transitions to capped-profit model

2020–2021  Standards & Generative AI

2020

  • OpenAI releases GPT-3 — NLP capabilities leap forward
  • DeepMind's AlphaFold 2 solves protein folding
  • IBM, Amazon, Microsoft pause facial recognition sales to police
  • AI Incident Database launched
  • UNESCO begins global AI ethics framework

2021

  • UNESCO adopts Recommendation on the Ethics of AI — first global standard Governance
  • EU releases draft AI Act
  • Google fires Timnit Gebru — diversity & ethics debate Risk
  • OpenAI releases DALL-E — text-to-image generation Tech
  • GitHub launches Copilot — AI coding assistant Tech

2022  The GenAI Explosion

  • DALL-E 2, Stable Diffusion (open source), and Midjourney launch publicly — generative image revolution begins. Tech Risk
  • White House publishes Blueprint for an AI Bill of Rights. Governance
  • Canada's AI & Data Act (AIDA); UK National AI Strategy.
  • NIST begins AI Risk Management Framework.
November 2022  ·  ChatGPT launches Tech
1 million users in 5 days. Fastest-growing consumer app in history. Transforms public awareness of AI overnight.

2023  Regulation & Safety

  • Mar FLI open letter calls for 6-month pause on AI > GPT-4 — signed by thousands including Musk & Bengio. Risk
  • Mar Italy temporarily bans ChatGPT (GDPR). Governance
  • — NIST releases the AI Risk Management Framework.
  • — OpenAI releases GPT-4, advancing multimodal reasoning. Tech
  • Oct Biden signs Executive Order on Safe, Secure & Trustworthy AI — most comprehensive U.S. AI policy to date. Governance
  • Nov First global AI Safety Summit at Bletchley Park — Bletchley Declaration signed by 28 countries. Governance
  • Nov OpenAI governance crisis — Altman fired, reinstated days later. Exposes tensions between safety and commercialisation. Risk

2024  AI Goes Everywhere

Models & Products

  • OpenAI releases GPT-4o — real-time multimodal AI
  • Google releases Gemini; OpenAI releases Sora (video)
  • OpenAI o1 reasoning models launch
  • Apple Intelligence in iOS 18 / macOS Sequoia
  • Tesla FSD v13; Google NotebookLM
  • xAI open-sources Grok

Governance & Society

  • EU AI Act enters into force Governance
  • Nvidia dominates AI chips; competitors accelerate
  • AI in drug discovery (Insitro), waste sorting, forensics
  • Nobel Prizes for AI: Hinton & Hopfield (Physics); Hassabis, Jumper, Baker (Chemistry) Tech

2025  Age of Agents

Models

  • DeepSeek-R1 — open-source, rivals GPT-4 at a fraction of cost Risk
  • OpenAI o3 / o3-mini; GPT-4.5
  • Google Gemini 2.5 Pro
  • Anthropic Claude 3.7 Sonnet with extended thinking
  • Meta Llama 4 (Scout & Maverick) — open-source multimodal
  • xAI Grok 3

Trends

  • OpenAI Operator — first mainstream AI agent
  • Agentic AI — plan, use tools, act autonomously
  • "Vibe coding" — AI-assisted programming goes mainstream
  • EU AI Act first enforcement milestone (Feb)
  • U.S. AI policy debates: export controls, safety evals
  • Global convergence on structured AI oversight

Key Themes Across the Timeline

Capability Cycles

  • Two AI Winters
  • Each followed by a bigger wave
  • Hype vs. results is a recurring tension

Governance Lag

  • Ethics frameworks follow capability
  • GDPR → OECD → AI Act → EO
  • Governance is now accelerating

Democratisation

  • TensorFlow → open-source models
  • GPT-2 withheld → Llama 4 released
  • Access vs. safety trade-off grows
